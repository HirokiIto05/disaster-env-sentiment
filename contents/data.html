Outcome Variables

  1. Environmental Tone in Congressional Speeches

    Congressional floor speeches from the U.S. House of Representatives are classified into three categories, including positive, negative, and neutral for environment protection, based on human-annotated training data and a ClimateBERT-based classifier fine-tuned for this task.

    The reference standard for classification is the League of Conservation Voters (LCV) Congressional Scorecard and ****: a speech segment is pro-environment if it advocates positions that the LCV would score favorably (e.g., supporting Clean Air Act regulations, renewable energy investment, or climate action), and anti-environment if it advocates positions the LCV would score unfavorably (e.g., opposing EPA rules, promoting fossil fuel production without environmental caveats, or dismissing climate science). Segments that describe policy without taking a position are coded as neutral.

    Annotation is restricted to topics tied to public attention and LCV voting: Clean Air, Clean Energy, Clean Water, Climate Change, Wildlife,and Dirty Energy. Other LCV categories (e.g., Toxics, Public Lands) are out of scope. For the detailed annotation criteria, see the Annotation Guideline.

    The outcome variable is the tone score: the share of positive speeches minus the share of negative speeches among all environment-related speeches per member per Congress, following Card et al. (2022).

    \[\text{Tone Score}_{i,t} = \frac{\text{Positive}_{i,t} - \text{Negative}_{i,t}} {\text{Positive}_{i,t} + \text{Negative}_{i,t} + \text{Neutral}_{i,t}}\]

    The score ranges from \(-1\) (all speeches anti-environment) to \(+1\) (all speeches pro-environment). It is designed to be comparable to the LCV score in direction: members with higher LCV scores are expected to have higher tone scores.

    Source: Congressional Record parsed via Hein-Bound corpora (gentzkow_congressional_nodate?)

    1.1 ClimateBERT Classifier

    Speech segments are classified using ClimateBERT (webersinke_climatebert_2022?), a domain-specific language model pretrained on climate-related text. The classification pipeline follows Card et al. (2022) in two stages: (1) a relevance classifier identifies whether a segment discusses one of the five LCV categories; (2) a tone classifier assigns pro-environment, anti-environment, or neutral labels to relevant segments. Both classifiers are fine-tuned on human annotations collected under the guideline above.

    Source: ClimateBERT: A Pretrained Language Model for Climate-Related Text (webersinke_climatebert_2022?)
